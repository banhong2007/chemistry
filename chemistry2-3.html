Chemistry MCQs: Structure and Properties of Materials, Ionic Bonding, and Covalent Bonding

Structure and Properties of Materials

Question 1: What is an element?

A) A substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances
B) A substance made of two or more elements chemically combined
C) A combination of two or more substances physically mixed
D) A substance that can be broken down into simpler substances

Answer: A
Explanation: An element is a pure substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means.

Question 2: What is a compound?

A) A substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances
B) A substance made of two or more elements chemically combined
C) A combination of two or more substances physically mixed
D) A substance that can be broken down into simpler substances

Answer: B
Explanation: A compound is a substance made of two or more elements chemically combined in fixed proportions.

Question 3: What is a mixture?

A) A substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances
B) A substance made of two or more elements chemically combined
C) A combination of two or more substances physically mixed
D) A substance that can be broken down into simpler substances

Answer: C
Explanation: A mixture is a combination of two or more substances that are physically mixed but not chemically combined.

Ionic Bonding

Question 4: How is a positive ion formed?

A) By gaining electrons
B) By losing electrons
C) By sharing electrons
D) By gaining protons

Answer: B
Explanation: A positive ion is formed by losing electrons, resulting in more protons than electrons in the atom.

Question 5: How is a negative ion formed?

A) By gaining electrons
B) By losing electrons
C) By sharing electrons
D) By losing protons

Answer: A
Explanation: A negative ion is formed by gaining electrons, resulting in more electrons than protons in the atom.

Question 6: What is an ionic bond?

A) A bond formed by sharing electrons
B) A bond formed by the transfer of electrons
C) A bond formed by the sharing of protons
D) A bond formed by the transfer of protons

Answer: B
Explanation: An ionic bond is formed by the transfer of electrons from one atom to another, resulting in the formation of positive and negative ions.

Question 7: Which of the following compounds is formed by ionic bonding?

A) H2O
B) CO2
C) NaCl
D) CH4

Answer: C
Explanation: NaCl (sodium chloride) is formed by ionic bonding between sodium (Na) and chlorine (Cl) atoms.

Question 8: What type of ions do metals typically form?

A) Positive ions
B) Negative ions
C) Neutral atoms
D) Molecules

Answer: A
Explanation: Metals typically form positive ions by losing electrons.

Question 9: What type of ions do non-metals typically form?

A) Positive ions
B) Negative ions
C) Neutral atoms
D) Molecules

Answer: B
Explanation: Non-metals typically form negative ions by gaining electrons.

Question 10: Which of the following pairs will form an ionic bond?

A) Hydrogen and Oxygen
B) Sodium and Chlorine
C) Carbon and Hydrogen
D) Nitrogen and Oxygen

Answer: B
Explanation: Sodium (a metal) and chlorine (a non-metal) will form an ionic bond.

Question 11: How are the physical properties of ionic compounds related to their lattice structure?

A) Ionic compounds have low melting and boiling points
B) Ionic compounds conduct electricity in solid state
C) Ionic compounds have high melting and boiling points
D) Ionic compounds are malleable and ductile

Answer: C
Explanation: Ionic compounds have high melting and boiling points due to the strong electrostatic forces of attraction between ions in their lattice structure.

Question 12: Why do ionic compounds conduct electricity when molten or dissolved in water?

A) Because the ions are free to move
B) Because the electrons are free to move
C) Because the protons are free to move
D) Because the neutrons are free to move

Answer: A
Explanation: Ionic compounds conduct electricity when molten or dissolved in water because the ions are free to move and carry an electric current.

Covalent Bonding

Question 13: How is a covalent bond formed?

A) By transferring electrons
B) By sharing a pair of electrons
C) By transferring protons
D) By sharing protons

Answer: B
Explanation: A covalent bond is formed by sharing a pair of electrons between two non-metal atoms.

Question 14: Which of the following molecules is formed by covalent bonding?

A) NaCl
B) MgO
C) H2O
D) KCl

Answer: C
Explanation: H2O (water) is formed by covalent bonding between hydrogen and oxygen atoms.

Question 15: What is the term for a pair of shared electrons in a covalent bond?

A) Ionic pair
B) Covalent pair
C) Electron pair
D) Proton pair

Answer: B
Explanation: A pair of shared electrons in a covalent bond is called a covalent pair.

Question 16: Which diagram represents the formation of a covalent bond in a hydrogen molecule (H2)?

A) H-H
B) H:H
C) H+H
D) H*H

Answer: B
Explanation: The diagram H:H represents the sharing of a pair of electrons (one from each hydrogen atom) in a covalent bond.

Question 17: Which of the following molecules is not formed by covalent bonding?

A) O2
B) CO2
C) CH4
D) NaCl

Answer: D
Explanation: NaCl (sodium chloride) is formed by ionic bonding, not covalent bonding.

Question 18: What is the maximum number of covalent bonds an atom of carbon can form?

A) 2
B) 3
C) 4
D) 5

Answer: C
Explanation: An atom of carbon can form a maximum of 4 covalent bonds to achieve a stable electronic configuration.

Question 19: How many covalent bonds are there in a molecule of methane (CH4)?

A) 2
B) 3
C) 4
D) 5

Answer: C
Explanation: There are 4 covalent bonds in a molecule of methane (CH4), one between carbon and each hydrogen atom.

Question 20: What is the electronic configuration of an oxygen atom in a water molecule (H2O)?

A) 2, 4
B) 2, 6
C) 2, 8
D) 2, 7

Answer: B
Explanation: The electronic configuration of an oxygen atom in a water molecule is 2, 6, as it shares two electrons with hydrogen atoms.

Question 21: Which molecule is represented by the dot and cross diagram O=O?

A) H2O
B) O2
C) CO2
D) CH4

Answer: B
Explanation: The dot and cross diagram O=O represents the double bond between two oxygen atoms in an O2 molecule.

Question 22: How many electrons are shared in a double covalent bond?

A) 2
B) 4
C) 6
D) 8

Answer: B
Explanation: A double covalent bond involves the sharing of 4 electrons (2 pairs) between two atoms.

Question 23: Which of the following statements about covalent bonds is true?

A) Covalent bonds are formed by the transfer of electrons
B) Covalent bonds are formed by sharing electrons
C) Covalent bonds involve the transfer of protons
D) Covalent bonds are weaker than van der Waals forces

Answer: B
Explanation: Covalent bonds are formed by the sharing of electrons between atoms.

Question 24: How many covalent bonds are there in a molecule of carbon dioxide (CO2)?

A) 1
B) 2
C) 3
D) 4

Answer: D
Explanation: There are 4 covalent bonds in a molecule of carbon dioxide (CO2), with two double bonds between carbon and each oxygen atom.

Question 25: Which of the following molecules has a triple covalent bond?

A) H2
B) O2
C) N2
D) CH4

Answer: C
Explanation: A molecule of nitrogen (N2) has a triple covalent bond between the two nitrogen atoms.

Question 26: What is the shape of a methane (CH4) molecule?

A) Linear
B) Bent
C) Trigonal planar
D) Tetrahedral

Answer: D
Explanation: A methane (CH4) molecule has a tetrahedral shape with bond angles of approximately 109.5°.

Question 27: Which type of bonding is present in a molecule of oxygen (O2)?

A) Ionic bonding
B) Covalent bonding
C) Metallic bonding
D) Hydrogen bonding

Answer: B
Explanation: Covalent bonding is present in a molecule of oxygen (O2), where two oxygen atoms share a pair of electrons.

Question 28: Which of the following is a property of covalent compounds?

A) High melting and boiling points
B) Conduct electricity when molten
C) Usually insoluble in water
D) Form crystal lattices

Answer: C
Explanation: Covalent compounds are usually insoluble in water and have low melting and boiling points. They do not conduct electricity.

Question 29: Which of the following molecules has a bent shape?

A) CO2
B) H2O
C) CH4
D) N2

Answer: B
Explanation: A water (H2O) molecule has a bent shape due to the two lone pairs of electrons on the oxygen atom.

Question 30: Which of the following properties is not characteristic of ionic compounds?

A) High melting and boiling points
B) Conduct electricity when dissolved in water
C) Malleable and ductile
D) Form crystal lattices

Answer: C
Explanation: Ionic compounds are not malleable and ductile; they are brittle and form crystal lattices.

Question 31: How many pairs of electrons are shared in a single covalent bond?

A) 1
B) 2
C) 3
D) 4

Answer: A
Explanation: A single covalent bond involves the sharing of one pair of electrons between two atoms.

Question 32: Which of the following substances contains both ionic and covalent bonds?

A) NaCl
B) H2O
C) NH4Cl
D) O2

Answer: C
Explanation: Ammonium chloride (NH4Cl) contains both ionic (NH4+ and Cl-) and covalent (N-H) bonds.

Question 33: Which type of bonding is found in a diamond?

A) Ionic bonding
B) Covalent bonding
C) Metallic bonding
D) Hydrogen bonding

Answer: B
Explanation: Diamond has a covalent bonding where each carbon atom forms four covalent bonds with other carbon atoms in a tetrahedral arrangement.

Question 34: What is the shape of a carbon dioxide (CO2) molecule?

A) Linear
B) Bent
C) Trigonal planar
D) Tetrahedral

Answer: A
Explanation: A carbon dioxide (CO2) molecule has a linear shape with bond angles of 180°.

Question 35: Which type of substance conducts electricity only when melted or dissolved in water?

A) Ionic compounds
B) Covalent compounds
C) Metals
D) Non-metals

Answer: A
Explanation: Ionic compounds conduct electricity when melted or dissolved in water because their ions are free to move and carry an electric current.

Question 36: Which of the following is not a property of covalent compounds?

A) Low melting and boiling points
B) Usually insoluble in water
C) Poor electrical conductivity
D) Good electrical conductivity when dissolved in water

Answer: D
Explanation: Covalent compounds generally do not conduct electricity well even when dissolved in water.

Question 37: Which molecule is represented by the dot and cross diagram H:H?

A) H2O
B) H2
C) CO2
D) CH4

Answer: B
Explanation: The dot and cross diagram H:H represents the sharing of a pair of electrons (one from each hydrogen atom) in a covalent bond, forming a hydrogen molecule (H2).

Question 38: Which of the following pairs of elements is most likely to form an ionic bond?

A) Carbon and Hydrogen
B) Nitrogen and Oxygen
C) Sodium and Fluorine
D) Phosphorus and Chlorine

Answer: C
Explanation: Sodium (a metal) and fluorine (a non-metal) are most likely to form an ionic bond.

Question 39: Which of the following substances has a giant covalent structure?

A) NaCl
B) H2O
C) Diamond
D) CO2

Answer: C
Explanation: Diamond has a giant covalent structure where each carbon atom is covalently bonded to four other carbon atoms.

Question 40: How many electrons are shared in a triple covalent bond?

A) 2
B) 4
C) 6
D) 8

Answer: C
Explanation: A triple covalent bond involves the sharing of 6 electrons (3 pairs) between two atoms.
