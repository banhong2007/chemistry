Chemistry MCQs: Methods of Purification and Analysis

Question 1: Which method is suitable for separating an insoluble solid from a liquid?

A) Filtration
B) Distillation
C) Paper chromatography
D) Crystallisation

Answer: A
Explanation: Filtration is used to separate an insoluble solid from a liquid. The solid particles are trapped by the filter paper, while the liquid passes through as the filtrate.

Question 2: Which method is used to separate a dissolved solid from a solution by heating?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Distillation
D) Paper chromatography

Answer: B
Explanation: Evaporation is used to separate a dissolved solid from a solution by heating the solution until the solvent evaporates, leaving the solid behind.

Question 3: What is the main purpose of crystallisation in separation processes?

A) To separate insoluble solids from liquids
B) To separate mixtures of liquids
C) To obtain pure solid crystals from a solution
D) To separate components based on their boiling points

Answer: C
Explanation: Crystallisation is used to obtain pure solid crystals from a solution. As the solvent evaporates or cools, the solute forms crystals.

Question 4: Which technique is used to separate mixtures of liquids with different boiling points?

A) Filtration
B) Paper chromatography
C) Crystallisation
D) Distillation

Answer: D
Explanation: Distillation is used to separate mixtures of liquids with different boiling points. The liquid with the lower boiling point evaporates first and is then condensed back into a liquid.

Question 5: What is the main difference between distillation and fractional distillation?

A) The use of filter paper
B) The type of mixture separated
C) The presence of a fractionating column
D) The use of a suitable solvent

Answer: C
Explanation: Fractional distillation involves the use of a fractionating column, which allows for the separation of liquids with closer boiling points by providing multiple condensation and evaporation stages.

Question 6: Which method is used to separate and analyze the components of a mixture based on their movement through a medium?

A) Filtration
B) Paper chromatography
C) Crystallisation
D) Distillation

Answer: B
Explanation: Paper chromatography is used to separate and analyze the components of a mixture based on their movement through a stationary medium (paper) and their differing affinities for the mobile phase (solvent).

Question 7: What is the role of the solvent in paper chromatography?

A) To dissolve the solute
B) To filter the mixture
C) To carry the components of the mixture up the paper
D) To crystallize the solute

Answer: C
Explanation: In paper chromatography, the solvent (mobile phase) carries the components of the mixture up the paper (stationary phase), allowing them to be separated based on their differing affinities for the paper and solvent.

Question 8: Which method is used to purify a solid that is soluble in a hot solvent but not in a cold solvent?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Crystallisation
D) Distillation

Answer: C
Explanation: Crystallisation is used to purify a solid that is soluble in a hot solvent but not in a cold solvent. The solid is dissolved in the hot solvent and then allowed to crystallize as the solution cools.

Question 9: Which method is suitable for separating a mixture of sand and salt?

A) Filtration and evaporation
B) Distillation and crystallisation
C) Paper chromatography and filtration
D) Filtration and distillation

Answer: A
Explanation: To separate a mixture of sand and salt, filtration is used to separate the sand (insoluble) from the salt solution (soluble), and then evaporation is used to obtain the salt from the solution.

Question 10: Which technique would you use to separate a liquid-liquid mixture where one liquid is immiscible with the other?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Distillation
D) Using a separating funnel

Answer: D
Explanation: A separating funnel is used to separate immiscible liquids (liquids that do not mix), such as oil and water, based on their differing densities.

Question 11: Which method involves heating a solution until the solvent evaporates, leaving the solute behind?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Crystallisation
D) Distillation

Answer: B
Explanation: Evaporation involves heating a solution until the solvent evaporates, leaving the solute behind. This method is used to separate dissolved solids from a solution.

Question 12: What is the purpose of a fractionating column in fractional distillation?

A) To filter the mixture
B) To provide multiple condensation and evaporation stages
C) To dissolve the solute
D) To carry the components up the paper

Answer: B
Explanation: A fractionating column in fractional distillation provides multiple condensation and evaporation stages, allowing for the separation of liquids with closer boiling points.

Question 13: Which method is used to separate colored components of a mixture based on their different solubilities?

A) Filtration
B) Paper chromatography
C) Crystallisation
D) Distillation

Answer: B
Explanation: Paper chromatography is used to separate colored components of a mixture based on their different solubilities and affinities for the stationary phase (paper) and the mobile phase (solvent).

Question 14: What is the main purpose of distillation in separation processes?

A) To separate insoluble solids from liquids
B) To separate mixtures of liquids based on their boiling points
C) To obtain pure solid crystals from a solution
D) To separate components based on their movement through a medium

Answer: B
Explanation: The main purpose of distillation is to separate mixtures of liquids based on their boiling points. The liquid with the lower boiling point evaporates first and is then condensed back into a liquid.

Question 15: Which method would you use to separate a mixture of ink dyes?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Paper chromatography
D) Distillation

Answer: C
Explanation: Paper chromatography is used to separate a mixture of ink dyes. The different dyes move up the paper at different rates based on their solubility in the solvent and their affinity for the paper.

Question 16: What is the main advantage of using crystallisation for purification?

A) It is faster than other methods
B) It provides a high yield of pure solid crystals
C) It does not require heating
D) It can separate mixtures of liquids

Answer: B
Explanation: Crystallisation provides a high yield of pure solid crystals by allowing the solute to form crystals as the solvent evaporates or cools, leaving impurities behind in the solution.

Question 17: Which method is suitable for separating a mixture of alcohol and water?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Crystallisation
D) Distillation

Answer: D
Explanation: Distillation is suitable for separating a mixture of alcohol and water based on their different boiling points. Alcohol, having a lower boiling point, will evaporate first and can be condensed and collected separately.

Question 18: Which method is used to separate an insoluble solid from a liquid by pouring the liquid off the top?

A) Filtration
B) Decanting
C) Crystallisation
D) Distillation

Answer: B
Explanation: Decanting is used to separate an insoluble solid from a liquid by carefully pouring the liquid off the top, leaving the solid behind.

Question 19: Which method is suitable for obtaining salt from seawater?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Paper chromatography
D) Distillation

Answer: B
Explanation: Evaporation is suitable for obtaining salt from seawater by heating the seawater until the water evaporates, leaving the salt behind.

Question 20: Which method would you use to separate a mixture of two miscible liquids with close boiling points?

A) Filtration
B) Fractional distillation
C) Crystallisation
D) Paper chromatography

Answer: B
Explanation: Fractional distillation is used to separate a mixture of two miscible liquids with close boiling points by providing multiple condensation and evaporation stages in a fractionating column, allowing for better separation.
