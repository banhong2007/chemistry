Chemistry MCQs: Separation and Purification Methods

Question 1: Which method is suitable for separating a mixture of iron filings and sulfur powder?

A) Filtration
B) Magnetic separation
C) Distillation
D) Crystallisation

Answer: B
Explanation: Magnetic separation is suitable for separating a mixture of iron filings (which are magnetic) from sulfur powder (which is not magnetic) using a magnet.

Question 2: Which method would you use to separate a mixture of salt and sand?

A) Filtration and evaporation
B) Distillation and crystallisation
C) Paper chromatography and filtration
D) Filtration and distillation

Answer: A
Explanation: To separate a mixture of salt (soluble) and sand (insoluble), filtration is used to separate the sand from the salt solution, and then evaporation is used to obtain the salt from the solution.

Question 3: What method is used to separate a mixture of sand and water?

A) Filtration
B) Distillation
C) Paper chromatography
D) Crystallisation

Answer: A
Explanation: Filtration is used to separate an insoluble solid (sand) from a liquid (water). The sand is trapped by the filter paper, while the water passes through as the filtrate.

Question 4: Which method is suitable for separating a mixture of ethanol and water?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Distillation
D) Paper chromatography

Answer: C
Explanation: Distillation is suitable for separating a mixture of ethanol and water based on their different boiling points. Ethanol, having a lower boiling point, will evaporate first and can be condensed and collected separately.

Question 6: What method is used to separate a mixture of oil and water?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Distillation
D) Using a separating funnel

Answer: D
Explanation: A separating funnel is used to separate immiscible liquids, such as oil and water, based on their differing densities. The denser liquid (water) can be drained off from the bottom, leaving the less dense liquid (oil) behind.

Question 7: Which method is suitable for separating a mixture of copper sulfate crystals and water?

A) Filtration
B) Evaporation
C) Distillation
D) Crystallisation

Answer: D
Explanation: Crystallisation is used to separate dissolved copper sulfate from water by allowing the water to evaporate slowly, forming pure copper sulfate crystals.
