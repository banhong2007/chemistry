Chemistry MCQs: Methods of Gas Collection

Question 1: Which method is suitable for collecting a gas that is denser than air?

A) Downward displacement of air
B) Upward displacement of air
C) Over water
D) Using a gas syringe

Answer: A
Explanation: Downward displacement of air is used to collect gases that are denser than air, such as carbon dioxide. This method takes advantage of the gas's higher density to settle at the bottom and displace lighter air upwards.

Question 2: For collecting a gas that is less dense than air, you would use:

A) Downward displacement of air
B) Upward displacement of air
C) Over water
D) Using a gas syringe

Answer: B
Explanation: Upward displacement of air is used to collect gases that are less dense than air, such as hydrogen. This method utilizes the gas's lower density, causing it to rise and displace the air in the container upwards.

Question 3: Which method is appropriate for collecting a gas that is soluble in water?

A) Over water
B) Using a gas syringe
C) Upward displacement of air
D) Downward displacement of air

Answer: B
Explanation: Gases that are soluble in water, such as ammonia, cannot be collected over water as they would dissolve. A gas syringe is suitable for collecting such gases, ensuring accurate collection without loss due to solubility.

Question 4: A gas that is insoluble in water can be collected by:

A) Upward displacement of air
B) Downward displacement of air
C) Over water
D) Using a gas syringe

Answer: C
Explanation: Gases that are insoluble in water, such as oxygen, can be collected by the displacement of water. This method involves bubbling the gas through water into an inverted container filled with water, where the gas displaces the water and is collected.
