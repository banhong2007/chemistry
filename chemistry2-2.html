Chemistry MCQs: Atomic Structure

Question 1: What is the relative charge of a proton?

A) -1
B) 0
C) +1
D) +2

Answer: C
Explanation: A proton has a relative charge of +1. It is positively charged.

Question 2: What is the relative charge of a neutron?

A) -1
B) 0
C) +1
D) +2

Answer: B
Explanation: A neutron has no charge; it is neutral.

Question 3: What is the relative charge of an electron?

A) -1
B) 0
C) +1
D) +2

Answer: A
Explanation: An electron has a relative charge of -1. It is negatively charged.

Question 4: What is the approximate relative mass of a proton?

A) 1
B) 0
C) 1/1840
D) 2

Answer: A
Explanation: A proton has an approximate relative mass of 1.

Question 5: What is the approximate relative mass of a neutron?

A) 1
B) 0
C) 1/1840
D) 2

Answer: A
Explanation: A neutron has an approximate relative mass of 1, similar to a proton.

Question 6: What is the approximate relative mass of an electron?

A) 1
B) 0
C) 1/1840
D) 2

Answer: C
Explanation: An electron has an approximate relative mass of 1/1840, which is much smaller than that of a proton or neutron.

Question 7: Which particles are found in the nucleus of an atom?

A) Protons and electrons
B) Neutrons and electrons
C) Protons and neutrons
D) Protons, neutrons, and electrons

Answer: C
Explanation: The nucleus of an atom contains protons and neutrons.

Question 8: Which particles are arranged in shells (energy levels) around the nucleus?

A) Protons
B) Neutrons
C) Electrons
D) Protons and neutrons

Answer: C
Explanation: Electrons are arranged in shells (energy levels) around the nucleus.

Question 9: What is the proton number (atomic number) of an element?

A) The number of neutrons in the nucleus
B) The number of protons in the nucleus
C) The total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus
D) The number of electrons in the outer shell

Answer: B
Explanation: The proton number (atomic number) of an element is the number of protons in the nucleus.

Question 10: What is the nucleon number (mass number) of an element?

A) The number of neutrons in the nucleus
B) The number of protons in the nucleus
C) The total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus
D) The number of electrons in the outer shell

Answer: C
Explanation: The nucleon number (mass number) of an element is the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus.

Question 11: What is the symbol for an isotope of carbon with 6 protons and 6 neutrons?

A) C14 6
B) C13 6
C) C12 6
D) C11 6

Answer: C
Explanation: The isotope of carbon with 6 protons and 6 neutrons is represented as C12 6, where 12 is the mass number (protons + neutrons) and 6 is the atomic number (protons).

Question 12: What is the term for atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons?

A) Ions
B) Isotopes
C) Molecules
D) Compounds

Answer: B
Explanation: Isotopes are atoms of the same element that have different numbers of neutrons.

Question 13: How many protons are in an atom of sodium (Na) with an atomic number of 11?

A) 10
B) 11
C) 12
D) 13

Answer: B
Explanation: An atom of sodium (Na) with an atomic number of 11 has 11 protons.

Question 14: How many neutrons are in an atom of chlorine (Cl) with an atomic number of 17 and a mass number of 35?

A) 17
B) 18
C) 35
D) 52

Answer: B
Explanation: The number of neutrons in an atom is the mass number minus the atomic number. For chlorine, it is 35 - 17 = 18.

Question 15: How many electrons are in a neutral atom of oxygen (O) with an atomic number of 8?

A) 6
B) 8
C) 10
D) 16

Answer: B
Explanation: A neutral atom has the same number of electrons as protons. An oxygen atom with an atomic number of 8 has 8 electrons.

Question 16: What is the term for the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom?

A) Atomic number
B) Mass number
C) Isotope number
D) Electron number

Answer: B
Explanation: The mass number is the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom.

Question 17: Which of the following symbols represents an isotope of hydrogen with 1 proton and 2 neutrons?

A) H1 1
B) H2 1
C) H3 1
D) H3 2

Answer: C
Explanation: The isotope of hydrogen with 1 proton and 2 neutrons is represented as H3 1, where 3 is the mass number and 1 is the atomic number.

Question 18: What does the number 12 in the symbol C12 6 represent?

A) The number of protons
B) The number of neutrons
C) The mass number
D) The atomic number

Answer: C
Explanation: The number 12 in the symbol C12 6 represents the mass number, which is the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus.

Question 19: How many protons, neutrons, and electrons are in a neutral atom of carbon-14 (C14 6)?

A) 6 protons, 6 neutrons, 6 electrons
B) 6 protons, 8 neutrons, 6 electrons
C) 7 protons, 7 neutrons, 7 electrons
D) 8 protons, 6 neutrons, 8 electrons

Answer: B
Explanation: A neutral atom of carbon-14 has 6 protons, 8 neutrons, and 6 electrons. The atomic number is 6 (protons), and the mass number is 14 (protons + neutrons).

Question 20: What is the term for a positively charged ion?

A) Anion
B) Cation
C) Neutron
D) Electron

Answer: B
Explanation: A positively charged ion is called a cation.

Question 21: Which element has an atomic number of 12 and is found in group 2 of the periodic table?

A) Sodium
B) Magnesium
C) Calcium
D) Beryllium

Answer: B
Explanation: Magnesium has an atomic number of 12 and is found in group 2 of the periodic table.

Question 22: How many electrons are in a chloride ion (Cl-) with an atomic number of 17?

A) 16
B) 17
C) 18
D) 19

Answer: C
Explanation: A chloride ion (Cl-) has gained one extra electron, so it has 18 electrons (17 protons + 1 extra electron).

Question 23: What is the mass number of an oxygen atom with 8 protons and 8 neutrons?

A) 8
B) 16
C) 24
D) 32

Answer: B
Explanation: The mass number is the total number of protons and neutrons. For this oxygen atom, it is 8 + 8 = 16.

Question 24: Which particle is found outside the nucleus of an atom?

A) Proton
B) Neutron
C) Electron
D) Nucleon

Answer: C
Explanation: Electrons are found outside the nucleus in shells or energy levels.

Question 25: Which element has 3 protons in its nucleus?

A) Helium
B) Lithium
C) Beryllium
D) Boron

Answer: B
Explanation: Lithium has 3 protons in its nucleus.

Question 26: What is the term for the weighted average mass of the isotopes of an element?

A) Atomic mass
B) Mass number
C) Atomic number
D) Isotopic mass

Answer: A
Explanation: The atomic mass is the weighted average mass of the isotopes of an element.

Question 27: Which of the following elements is a noble gas with an atomic number of 10?

A) Helium
B) Neon
C) Argon
D) Krypton

Answer: B
Explanation: Neon is a noble gas with an atomic number of 10.

Question 28: How many neutrons are in an isotope of nitrogen-15 (N15 7)?

A) 7
B) 8
C) 15
D) 22

Answer: B
Explanation: The number of neutrons is the mass number minus the atomic number. For nitrogen-15, it is 15 - 7 = 8.

Question 29: Which particle determines the identity of an element?

A) Proton
B) Neutron
C) Electron
D) Nucleon

Answer: A
Explanation: The number of protons determines the identity of an element.

Question 30: Which symbol represents an ion of calcium with 20 protons and 18 electrons?

A) Ca2+
B) Ca2-
C) Ca+
D) Ca-

Answer: A
Explanation: An ion of calcium with 20 protons and 18 electrons has lost 2 electrons, resulting in a 2+ charge.
